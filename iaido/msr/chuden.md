# 英信流 Eishin-ryū

> 是重信翁より段々相傳の居合然者を最初に教へべき筈のものなれ共先大森流は初心の者覚へ安き故に是を先に教ると云へり

This iai has been handed down in succession from Shigenobu. Although by rights it should be taught first, Ōmori-ryū is taught first because it is easier for beginners to learn.

## 横雲 Yokogumo

> 深山には　あらし吹くらし　みよし野の  
> 華か霞か　横雲のそら

Deep in the mountains, storm winds seem to blow;  
are they the blossoms of Yoshino, or mist, in a cloud-banded sky?

> 右足を向ふへ踏出し抜付打込み開き足を引て先に坐したる通りにして納る也

Step the right foot forwards, perform nukitsuke, strike in, open, draw the foot back, and sheathe as in the original seated posture.

## 虎一足 Tora-issoku

> 猛き虎の　千里の歩み　遠からず  
> 行より早く　歸るあし引

A fierce tiger covers a thousand *ri* as no great distance;  
swifter still than its going is its returning step.

> 左足を引刀を逆さまに抜て留め扨打込あと上におなし

Draw the left foot back, draw the sword upside down and check \[the enemy's sword\], then strike in; after that, same as above.

## 稲妻 Inazuma

> もろともに　光りと知れと　稲妻の  
> あと鳴雷の　響知られず

Though the flash is seen in an instant,  
the thunder that follows the lightning is never heard.

> 左あしを引敵の切って懸る拳を拂ろふて打込み跡上に同し

Draw the left foot back, sweep aside the fist that comes cutting in, then strike in; after that, same as above.

## 浮雲 Ukigumo

> 麓より　吹上られし　浮雲は  
> 四方の高根を　立包むなり

Floating clouds blown up from the foothills  
rise to envelop the high peaks on every side.

> 右へ振り向き足を踏みもぢ彳腰をひねり抜付左の手を添て敵を突たおす心にて右の足を上は拍子に刀を膝へ引切先を後ろへはね扨上へ冠膝の外へ打跡上に同し又刀を引て切先を跡へはねずして取って打込む業もあり

Turn to the right, plant the foot to assume a torsioned posture, and twist the hips into nukitsuke; add the left hand and, with the feeling of driving the opponent down, in the timing of raising the right foot draw the sword back toward the knee, flip the kissaki to the rear, then raise overhead and strike outside the knee; after that, same as above. There is also a form in which one draws and strikes in without flipping the kissaki to the rear.

## 山下風 Yamaoroshi

> 高根より　吹下す風の　强ければ  
> 麓の木々は　雪もたまらず

If the wind blowing down from the high peaks is strong,  
even snow does not stay on the trees below.

> 右へ振り向左の足と右の手を柄と一處にして打倒し抜付跡前の通り但足は右あし也浮雲とあしは相違也

Turn to the right; bring the left leg and the right hand together with the hilt, strike down and perform nukitsuke; thereafter as before. However, the foot used is the right foot; this differs from Ukigumo.

## 岩浪 Iwanami

> 行舟の　かちとり直す　間もなきは  
> 岩尾の浪の　強く当れば

A travelling boat has no time to reset the helm,  
when waves strike hard against the rocky reef.

> 左りへふり向左の足を引刀を抜左の手切先へ添へ右の膝の外ゟ突膝の内に引あとは山下風の業に同し

Turn to the left, draw the left foot back, and draw the sword; place the left hand along the blade toward the tip, thrust from outside the right knee, then draw in to the inside of the knee; after that, same as the waza of Yamaoroshi.

## 鱗返 Urokogaeshi

> 瀧津なみ　瀬登る鯉の　鱗は  
> 水関上て　落る叓なし

The scales of the carp climbing the rapids  
go up over the weir and do not fall back down.

> 右に向き居って左り廻りに向へ抜付左の足を引冠り打込み開き納る也

While seated facing to the right, turn to the left, perform nukitsuke, draw back the left foot, raise overhead, strike in, open, and sheathe.

## 浪返 Namigaeshi

> あかし潟　瀬戸越波の　上にこそ  
> 岩尾も岸も　たまるものかわ

The waves crossing the strait of Akashi —  
upon them, what cliff or shore could ever endure?

> 鱗返しに同し後へ抜付打込み開納る後ろへ廻と脇へ廻ると斗相違也

Same as Urokogaeshi, but perform the draw-cut to the rear, strike in, open, and sheathe; the only difference is turning to the rear rather than turning to the side.

## 瀧落 Takiotoshi

> 瀧津瀬の　崩るゝ叓の　深ければ  
> 前に立添ふ　岩もなきかな

When the waterfall plunges deep,  
not even a rock can stand before it.

> 刀の鞘と共に左の足と一拍子に出し抜て後を突すぐに右の足を踏込み打込み開き納る此業は後ろゟこじりを取りたる處也故に抜時にこじりに当る心持有り

With the sword and scabbard together, send out the left foot in a single beat, draw, and thrust to the rear; immediately step in with the right foot, strike in, open, and sheathe. This waza is for when the kojiri has been taken from behind; therefore, at the draw, keep the feeling of striking with the kojiri.

## 抜打 Nukiuchi

(Because Nukiuchi was appended to Eishin-ryū at a later stage, it does not have a corresponding poem.)

> 大森流の抜打に同し

This is the same as Ōmori-ryū Nukiuchi.

# Nakayama Hakudō Quotes on Eishin-ryū Forms (英信流居合之事)

Primary source in this corpus: 中山博道校閲・太田龍峰著『居合読本』(昭和9年, 1934), as quoted in the linked posts.

## 1) 横雲 (Yokogumo)

Japanese quote:
> 意義　大森流初発刀に同じである（初発刀の意義　互に四尺位離れて對坐せる時、急に敵の目の附近を横薙に切り付け、相抜きの場合は敵の抜付けし拳に斬り込む、倒るゝ所を直ちに上段より斬る業である。）  
> 動作　正面に向ひ箕坐す、右足を僅か前方に踏み出し大森流の初発刀と同様に抜刀して直ちに頭上に振りかぶり、敵を斬り下ろし、直ちに陰陽進退の最初の血振りをなし、刀を納めつゝ右足を左足に引き付けて蹲踞し、後、徐かに立ち上がる。

English translation:
> Meaning: It is the same as Ōmori-ryū Shohattō. (Meaning of Shohattō: when seated facing each other about four shaku apart, suddenly cut sideways near the enemy's eyes; in a simultaneous draw, cut into the enemy's drawing fist; as he falls, immediately cut from jōdan.)  
> Action: Face forwards in kiza, step the right foot slightly forwards, and draw as in Ōmori-ryū Shohattō; at once raise overhead and cut down the enemy; immediately perform the first chiburi of In'yō Shintai; while sheathing, draw the right foot to the left foot and squat, then slowly stand.

## 2) 乕一足 / 虎一足 (Toraissoku)

Japanese quote:
> （意義）敵が前方から我が右臂（みぎひじ）を斬って来るのを抜刀して之を受け、敵の退くに乗じ正面に向ひ斬る業である。  
> （動作）正面に向ひ箕坐す、刀柄を上から握り、半ば刀を抜きつゝ左足を後方に踏み開き、刀を右足の側方に刀刃を前方にして敵の斬りつける刀を拂ひ受け、刀を頭上に振り被りつゝ左足を右足に引きつけ、右足を僅かに前方に踏みつけて正面を斬り直ちに血振りをする。以下前に同じ。

English translation:
> Meaning: When an enemy cuts at my right arm (elbow area) from the front, I draw and receive it, then, taking advantage of the enemy's retreat, cut towards the front.  
> Action: Face forwards in kiza, grip the hilt from above, half-draw while stepping the left foot open to the rear, place the blade at the side of the right foot with the edge forwards and sweep-receive the enemy's cut, raise overhead while drawing the left foot to the right foot, step the right foot slightly forwards and cut to the front, then immediately perform chiburi. The rest is the same as before.

## 3) 稲妻 (Inazuma)

Japanese quote:
> （意義）前方から斬って来る敵之起り頭を乗じ其前臂(ひじ）を斬る業である。  
> （動作）正面に向ひ箕坐す。右足を左足に引きつけ左足を一歩ひきつゝ中腰の儘で抜刀して敵の前臂を切り（大森流の勢中刀を参照）左膝を右足に引きつけつゝ刀を頭上に振り冠り右足を前に踏みつけて正面を斬る。以下前に同じ。

English translation:
> Meaning: A technique in which, taking the enemy at the instant of his initiation when he cuts from the front, one cuts his forearm.  
> Action: Face forwards in kiza. Draw the right foot to the left foot, and while withdrawing the left foot one step, draw in a half-raised posture and cut the enemy's forearm (see Ōmori-ryū Seichūtō); while drawing the left knee to the right foot, raise the sword overhead and, stepping the right foot forwards, cut to the front. The rest is the same as before.

## 4) 浮雲 (Ukigumo)

Japanese quote:
> 意義　右側面に坐せる敵が我が刀柄を握ろうとするのを避けつゝ立ち上り抜刀と同時に斬り着け敵の倒るゝに乗じ胴部を斬る業である。  
> 動作　正面に對し左向に箕坐す。敵が我刀を握ろうとするのを、左手の拇指を鍔にかけ刀柄を左方に開きつゝ何心なき態で立ち上り、左足を右足の前方から廻して右足の外側に小趾の側にて踏みつゝ腰を右に捻り両膝を僅かに屈げ、之を左右に開きて平均を保つ、此の間左手で以って刀を少し上方に引き出しつゝ（敵の頭上に触れない様にする心持で）左手を右腰の附近に致し、後、概ね舊帯刀の位置に復せしめ、此の姿勢に於て右拳を以って刀を抜き、鯉口を離れる時、體が概ね正面に向ふ如く腰を左に捻りつゝ僅かに立ち上る心地にて斬りつける。  
> 茲に於て左足裏を全部踏み着けると同時に左手を物打附近の刀背部に當て右足を左足の僅かに右後ろに踏み開き両手を以て敵を押し倒し、且つ引き切る如き動作をなす。  
> 此際左膝は動作の基礎となるのであって、概ね直角より伸びないのが可い、又、右足踵を挙げ右膝は屈げて體を安定させることが肝要である。  
> 右膝を突くと同時に右臂を僅かに伸ばしつゝ刀を右前上方に上げ左手を以て柄頭を握り刀を頭上に振り被りつゝ右膝を軸として右足を僅かに左足の後方に旋廻し、次に左足を僅かに右に踏み着けると同時に斬り下ろす。血振りをなし刀を納め左足を右足に引き著け蹲踞し、後、徐かに立ち上る。

English translation:
> Meaning: When an enemy seated at my right side tries to seize my hilt, avoid him while rising, cut as the sword is drawn, and, taking advantage of the enemy's fall, cut the torso.  
> Action: Facing the front, sit in kiza facing left. As the enemy tries to seize my sword, place the left thumb on the tsuba and open the hilt to the left while rising as if unconcerned; move the left foot around in front of the right foot and set it to the outside of the right foot on the little-toe side, twist the hips right and lightly bend both knees, opening them left and right to keep balance. During this, draw the sword slightly upwards with the left hand (with the intention of not touching above the enemy's head), bring the left hand near the right hip, then return roughly to the former carrying position. From this posture, draw with the right fist and, as the sword leaves the koiguchi, twist the hips left so that the body roughly faces forwards and cut, with the feeling of rising slightly.  
> At this point, as the entire sole of the left foot is set down, place the left hand on the back of the blade near the monouchi, step the right foot slightly open to the right rear of the left foot, and with both hands perform an action like pushing the enemy down and drawing-cutting.  
> Here, the left knee is the basis of the action and should generally not extend beyond a right angle; also, it is essential to raise the right heel and bend the right knee to stabilise the body.  
> As the right knee thrusts, slightly extend the right arm and raise the sword to the right-front upper side; grip the kashira with the left hand and raise overhead; using the right knee as the axis, rotate the right foot slightly to the rear of the left foot, then, as the left foot is set slightly to the right, cut down. Perform chiburi, sheath the sword, draw the left foot to the right foot and squat, then slowly stand.

## 5) 山下風 (Yamaoroshi / Yamashitakaze in this transcription)

Japanese quote:
> 意義　右側面に坐せる敵が抜刀せんとするを取り敢えず刀柄を以て其の手背を強打しヒルム所を抜刀して斬りつけ、其の殪（たお）るゝを再び正面より胴部に向ひ斬り下ろす業である。  
> 動作　正面に対し左向きに箕坐す。左膝を軸として約九十度右に向くと同時に刀に反りを打たせつゝ左手を以って刀を少しく前上方に出し、右足を約一歩前方に踏み着くると同時に鍔を以って敵の手を打つ、次に左手を以て刀を上方より敵の頭を越えて敵にふれない心持ちで右に旋廻し、左拳を概ね右腰の附近より舊帯刀の位置に復しつゝ、右拳で刀柄を握り左膝左右足尖を軸として左足を右足に旋廻して、右足の後方に至らせ、腰を左に捻りつゝ抜刀して敵の胸に斬りつく。  
> 直ちに左膝及び右足尖を軸として左足を約九十度左に旋廻して、左手を刀尖に近き刀背に當て右脚を左膝附近に引き着け刀を右後ろに引きつゝ敵を切り倒す（刀を引く時身體に触れない様に注意するを要す）、其位置に於て刀を反転し右足を約一歩前方に踏み出しつゝ左手を添へ、右足尖を軸として、左足を約九十度右に旋廻し、概ね正面に向く間に全く刀を頭上に振り被り直に斬り下ろす、以下横雲に同じ。

English translation:
> Meaning: When an enemy seated at my right side tries to draw, first strike the back of his hand hard with the hilt; when he flinches, draw and cut him, then, as he falls, cut down again towards the torso from the front.  
> Action: Facing the front, sit in kiza facing left. Using the left knee as an axis, turn about ninety degrees right; at the same time give the sword its curvature and send it slightly forwards and upwards with the left hand, and as the right foot is stepped about one pace forwards, strike the enemy's hand with the tsuba. Next, with the left hand rotate the sword to the right from above, passing over the enemy's head with the intention of not touching him; while returning the left fist from near the right hip to the former carrying position, grip the hilt with the right fist, and using the left knee and both toes as axes rotate the left foot around to the right foot so that it comes behind the right foot; twist the hips left while drawing and cut into the enemy's chest.  
> Immediately, using the left knee and right toe as axes, rotate the left foot about ninety degrees left; place the left hand on the back of the blade near the tip, draw the right leg to near the left knee, and pull the sword to the right rear while cutting the enemy down (when pulling the sword, care is required not to touch the body). At that position, reverse the sword, step the right foot about one pace forwards while adding the left hand, and, using the right toe as an axis, rotate the left foot about ninety degrees right; while turning roughly to face the front, raise the sword fully overhead and cut down directly. The rest is the same as Yokogumo.

## 6) 岩浪 (Iwanami)

Japanese quote:
> 意義　吾が左側に接近して坐せる敵の季肋部（きろくぶ、肋骨の真下）を刺突し直ちに敵を引き倒して後、斬る業である。  
> 動作　正面に對し右向に箕坐す。上体を前に傾けると同時に左足を斜後方に引きつゝ抜刀し直ちに左手を刀尖に近き刀背に添へ、成るべく低く左向きをなしつゝ右足を左膝頭附近に踏みつけ敵の季肋部を突き刺す。この時、刀は左手を刀身の中央に至る位突き出す、次に右手を以て刀を左手が刀尖附近に来る位迄引き、左膝を軸として約九十度右向をなし、右足を左膝附近に引きつゝ両手を以て、刀を引き、敵をひき倒す。この際右臂は充分伸ばすものとす、以下、山下風に同じ。

English translation:
> Meaning: A technique in which one thrusts into the hypochondrium (just under the ribs) of an enemy seated close on one's left side, immediately pulls the enemy down, and then cuts.  
> Action: Facing the front, sit in kiza facing right. At the same time as leaning the upper body forwards, draw while withdrawing the left foot diagonally to the rear; immediately place the left hand on the back of the blade near the tip and, while keeping as low as possible and turning left, step the right foot near the left knee and thrust into the enemy's side under the ribs. At this point, thrust so that the left hand comes roughly to the middle of the blade. Next, with the right hand pull the sword back until the left hand comes near the tip; using the left knee as an axis turn about ninety degrees right; while drawing the right foot near the left knee, pull the sword with both hands and pull the enemy down. At this point the right arm should be fully extended. The rest is the same as Yamaoroshi.

## 7) 鱗返 (Urokogaeshi)

Japanese quote:
> 意義　大森流左刀に同じ。  
> 動作　正面に對し右向に坐す。上體を少しく前に傾け右膝の外側より右手を以て、刀柄を握り右足蹠骨部（足の裏の指の基部の骨）を軸として約九十度左向きをすると同時に左足を後方に約二足長半乃至三足長踏み開き、初発刀の如く抜刀す。此際左膝は地に着かざるものとす。以下横雲に同じである。

English translation:
> Meaning: The same as Ōmori-ryū Hidaritō.  
> Action: Facing the front, sit facing right. Lean the upper body slightly forwards and, from the outside of the right knee, grip the hilt with the right hand; using the metatarsal area of the right foot as an axis, turn about ninety degrees left, and at the same time step the left foot open to the rear by about two and a half to three foot-lengths, and draw as in Shohattō. At this point the left knee should not touch the ground. The rest is the same as Yokogumo.

## 8) 浪返 / 波返 (Namigaeshi)

Japanese quote:
> 意義　敵が我が後方から斬り来るに對し後ろに振り向きつゝ初発刀の如く斬る業である。  
> 動作　正面に對し後ろ向きに箕坐す。鱗返しの要領にて約百八十度左に旋廻するの外、全く同じである。

English translation:
> Meaning: A technique in which, against an enemy cutting from behind, one turns to the rear and cuts as in Shohattō.  
> Action: Facing the front, sit in kiza facing backwards. Other than turning about 180 degrees to the left in the manner of Urokogaeshi, it is entirely the same.

## 9) 瀧落 (Takiotoshi)

Japanese quote:
> 意義　敵が我が鐺を握ろうとするのを、之を避けて立ち上がったが、尚ほ追ひ迫るを以て再び之を避け、遂に抜刀して振り向きつゝ敵を突き刺し、尚ほ追撃する業である。  
> 動作　正面に對し後ろ向きに箕坐す。左手を以て刀柄を左方に開きつゝ立ち上り左足を一歩前に踏み出すと同時に左拳が概ね右肩の附近に来る位まで刀を鞘の儘、抜き出す、此際左踵は地に着かぬものとす。  
> 次に右手を以て刀柄を握り右足を左足の稍々前側方に踏み着けると同時に左手にて、鞘を下方に押し下げつゝ體に近く抜刀し、刀尖が概ね乳の上附近に来る如くす。此際後方から見た刀及び鞘の形は、概ね〇鞘の（直角の）如くなるものとす。  
> 右足を軸として、後ろ向きをなしつゝ左足を少しく前方に踏み出すと同時に刀刃を上にし、右片手にて敵を突き刺し、直ちに、刀を頭上に振り被り右足を左足の前方に踏み着けて、敵を斬り側方に血振をなしつゝ左膝を地につく以下横雲に同じ。

English translation:
> Meaning: An enemy tries to seize my kojiri; I avoid him and rise, but as he still presses in I avoid again, and finally draw, turn, thrust the enemy, and then continue the attack.  
> Action: Facing the front, sit in kiza facing backwards. With the left hand open the hilt to the left while rising; at the same time as stepping the left foot one pace forwards, draw the sword (still in the scabbard) until the left fist comes roughly near the right shoulder; at this point the left heel does not touch the ground.  
> Next, grip the hilt with the right hand and, at the same time as stepping the right foot slightly to the front-side of the left foot, push the scabbard down with the left hand and draw close to the body so that the tip comes roughly to around chest height. At this point, seen from behind, the shape of sword and scabbard should be roughly a right angle.  
> Using the right foot as an axis, while turning to the rear, step the left foot slightly forwards and at the same time turn the edge upward; thrust the enemy one-handed with the right hand; immediately raise overhead, step the right foot in front of the left foot, cut the enemy, perform side chiburi while dropping the left knee to the ground, and then it is the same as Yokogumo.

## 10) 抜打 (Nukiuchi)

Japanese quote:
> 意義　大森流に全く同じ。  
> 動作　大森流に全く同じ。

English translation:
> Meaning: Exactly the same as Ōmori-ryū.  
> Action: Exactly the same as Ōmori-ryū.

Referenced quote in the same post (Ōmori-ryū Nukiuchi):
> 意義　彼我互に接近して對坐せる時不意に正面に向ひ斬り付ける動作である。  
> 動作　正面に向ひ正坐す。彼我極めて接近しある場合を考慮せるものなるを以って抜刀に際しては成るべく右拳を前上方に向けて動かしつゝ、概ね前額の前方に至らしめ、刀尖を左上膊の外側に近く移動せしめつゝ刀を頭上に振り被る（此際両膝を密接す）次で、直ちに両膝を開き刀尖が概ね地より二握り位の處に来る位に切り下ろす。次いで左手を放ち右拳を右に開き血振りをなすこと陰陽進退（陽進陰退・八重垣）における第一段の血振りと同様に動作し、後、刀を納む。

Referenced quote translation:
> Meaning: When both are seated facing each other at very close range, this is the action of suddenly cutting towards the front.  
> Action: Sit in seiza facing forwards. Because this assumes very close distance, at the draw move the right fist as much as possible forwards and upwards to about in front of the forehead, move the tip close to the outside of the left upper arm, and raise the sword overhead (here both knees are kept close together). Then immediately open both knees and cut down so the tip comes to about two fist-widths above the ground. Next release the left hand and open the right fist to the right for chiburi, in the same way as the first-stage chiburi in In'yō Shintai (Yōshin Intai / Yaegaki), then sheath.
